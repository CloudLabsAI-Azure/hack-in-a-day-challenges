# Budget Request & Planning Process

**Fiscal Year 2024 Guide**  
**Finance Department**

## Overview

This guide outlines the budget planning cycle, request procedures, and approval workflows for department budget planning and in-year budget requests.

## Annual Budget Cycle

### Timeline

**July-August: Planning Phase**
- Finance distributes budget templates and guidelines
- Departments review prior year actuals and current year forecast
- Strategic initiatives identified for upcoming year
- Preliminary discussions with finance business partners

**September: Budget Development**
- Week 1-2: Department managers draft initial budgets
- Week 3: Review with department heads
- Week 4: Submit to finance for consolidation

**October: Review & Refinement**
- Week 1-2: Finance reviews and challenges submissions
- Week 3: Department revisions and resubmissions
- Week 4: Executive leadership review

**November: Approval**
- Week 1-2: Board review and feedback
- Week 3-4: Final adjustments and board approval

**December: Communication**
- Final budgets distributed to departments
- Budget loaded into financial systems
- Budget training for managers

**January 1: New Fiscal Year**
- Budgets take effect
- Monthly variance reporting begins

## Budget Components

### Operating Expenses

**Personnel Costs (60-70% of budget)**
- Base salaries for existing positions
- Planned salary increases (merit, promotion, market adjustments)
- New hire additions with justification
- Benefits and payroll taxes (loaded at 30% of salary)
- Contractor and temporary staff costs
- Overtime projections

**Technology & Software (10-15%)**
- Software licenses and subscriptions (SaaS)
- Hardware purchases and replacements
- IT support and maintenance
- Cloud computing costs
- Cybersecurity tools

**Professional Services (5-10%)**
- Consultants and advisors
- Legal services
- Accounting and audit fees
- Recruiting and staffing services
- Training and development

**Facilities & Operations (5-10%)**
- Office rent and utilities (allocated by headcount)
- Office supplies and equipment
- Furniture and fixtures
- Telecommunications
- Postage and shipping

**Travel & Entertainment (5-10%)**
- Business travel costs
- Client entertainment
- Conference attendance
- Team events and meals

**Marketing & Sales (varies by dept)**
- Advertising and promotion
- Events and trade shows
- Sales materials
- Market research
- Public relations

**Other Operating Costs**
- Dues and subscriptions
- Insurance
- Miscellaneous expenses

### Capital Expenditures

**Equipment & Hardware**
- Computers and peripherals ($1,000+ per item)
- Servers and network equipment
- Manufacturing equipment
- Vehicles

**Software & Systems**
- Enterprise software implementations
- Custom software development
- Major system upgrades

**Facilities & Improvements**
- Office renovations
- Leasehold improvements
- Furniture and fixtures

**Capitalization Threshold:**
- Individual items: $1,000+
- Useful life: 3+ years
- Depreciated over asset life

## Budget Request Requirements

### New Headcount Request

**Required Information:**
1. **Position Details**
   - Job title and level
   - Department and reporting structure
   - Full-time or part-time
   - Start date and ramp timeline

2. **Business Justification**
   - Specific business need or problem to solve
   - Impact if position not filled
   - Alternative solutions considered
   - Strategic alignment

3. **Financial Information**
   - Estimated salary range
   - Total compensation (salary + benefits)
   - Additional costs (equipment, software, travel)
   - Revenue impact or cost savings expected

4. **Resource Plan**
   - Workspace and equipment needs
   - Onboarding and training plan
   - Ongoing management and support

**Approval Requirements:**
- Department head approval
- VP approval
- CFO approval for director+ roles
- CEO approval for VP+ roles

### Technology Investment Request

**Required Information:**
1. **Solution Overview**
   - Technology or software description
   - Vendor information
   - Implementation timeline
   - Users/departments impacted

2. **Business Case**
   - Problem or opportunity addressed
   - Benefits and expected outcomes
   - Metrics to measure success
   - Risks if not implemented

3. **Cost Analysis**
   - One-time costs (licensing, implementation, training)
   - Recurring costs (subscriptions, maintenance, support)
   - 3-year total cost of ownership
   - FTE costs for implementation and ongoing support

4. **Alternatives Considered**
   - Other vendors or solutions evaluated
   - Build vs. buy analysis
   - Do nothing alternative

5. **Implementation Plan**
   - Project timeline and milestones
   - Resources required
   - Change management approach
   - Risk mitigation

**Approval Requirements:**
- Department head approval
- IT architecture review
- CFO approval (>$50K)
- Executive committee approval (>$250K)

### Marketing Campaign Request

**Required Information:**
1. **Campaign Overview**
   - Campaign objectives and target audience
   - Channels and tactics
   - Timeline and duration
   - Creative and messaging approach

2. **Budget Breakdown**
   - Media costs (digital, print, outdoor)
   - Agency and creative fees
   - Production costs
   - Technology and tools
   - Event costs if applicable

3. **Expected Outcomes**
   - Lead generation targets
   - Brand awareness metrics
   - Conversion rate assumptions
   - Revenue impact projections

4. **Measurement Plan**
   - KPIs and success metrics
   - Tracking and attribution approach
   - Reporting cadence

**Approval Requirements:**
- Marketing director approval
- CMO approval
- CFO approval (>$100K)

### Capital Expenditure Request

**Required Information:**
1. **Asset Description**
   - Type of asset
   - Specifications and features
   - Vendor and pricing
   - Useful life

2. **Justification**
   - Business need
   - Replacement vs. expansion
   - Impact on productivity or revenue
   - Required vs. nice-to-have

3. **Financial Analysis**
   - Purchase cost vs. lease/rent
   - ROI calculation
   - Payback period
   - Ongoing maintenance costs

4. **Alternatives**
   - Repair existing equipment
   - Lease or rent options
   - Different vendors or models

**Approval Requirements:**
- Department head approval
- Finance approval
- CFO approval ($10K+)
- Executive committee approval ($100K+)

## In-Year Budget Requests

### When Required
- Expenses not included in annual budget
- Costs exceeding budgeted amounts by >10%
- New initiatives or opportunities arising mid-year
- Response to competitive threats or market changes

### Process
1. **Assess Budget Availability**
   - Check current budget vs. actuals in financial system
   - Identify potential offsets within department budget
   - Consult with finance business partner

2. **Prepare Business Case**
   - Use standard budget request template
   - Provide compelling justification
   - Explain urgency and timing
   - Quantify expected benefits

3. **Submit Request**
   - Submit through budget request system: budget.company.com
   - Attach supporting documentation
   - Route for appropriate approvals

4. **Review & Decision**
   - Finance reviews within 1 week
   - May request additional information
   - Approval/denial communicated with rationale
   - Approved requests added to budget

### Approval Thresholds

**Department Manager:**
- Up to $5,000 (within department budget)

**Department Director:**
- Up to $25,000 (with budget offsets)

**VP/Department Head:**
- Up to $100,000 (with strong business case)

**CFO:**
- Up to $500,000

**CEO:**
- Over $500,000

**Board of Directors:**
- Over $2,000,000 or >5% of budget

## Budget Monitoring & Reporting

### Monthly Budget Reviews

**Week 1 of Each Month:**
- Finance closes prior month
- Actuals loaded to financial system
- Variance reports generated

**Week 2:**
- Department managers review reports
- Identify and explain variances >10%
- Forecast updated for remainder of year

**Week 3:**
- Department head reviews with team
- Finance business partner discusses trends
- Action plans for unfavorable variances

**Week 4:**
- Executive team receives consolidated report
- Finance presents key insights and concerns
- Decisions made on course corrections

### Variance Explanations Required For:
- Any line item >10% over/under budget
- Any new expenses not budgeted
- Timing differences affecting multiple months
- One-time vs. recurring expense clarifications

### Forecasting Updates

**Quarterly Reforecasting:**
- Update remaining months based on actuals and new information
- Identify risks and opportunities
- Adjust annual projections
- Submit to finance by 15th day of quarter

**Rolling Forecasts:**
- Some departments use rolling 12-month forecasts
- Updated monthly with actuals
- Focuses on next 4-6 quarters

## Budget Best Practices

### Planning Tips
1. **Start with Prior Year**
   - Review last year's budget vs. actuals
   - Understand variances and lessons learned
   - Carry forward relevant trends

2. **Build Bottom-Up**
   - Detail all known expenses
   - Get input from team members
   - Validate assumptions with data

3. **Plan for Growth**
   - Factor in inflation (typically 3-5%)
   - Account for volume increases
   - Include strategic initiatives

4. **Be Realistic**
   - Don't lowball to appear efficient
   - Don't pad excessively for cushion
   - Use historical data to support estimates

5. **Document Assumptions**
   - Note key assumptions driving numbers
   - Explain growth rates and drivers
   - Provide context for reviewers

### Ongoing Management

1. **Monitor Monthly**
   - Review actuals vs. budget regularly
   - Identify trends early
   - Take corrective action promptly

2. **Communicate Changes**
   - Alert finance to material changes
   - Explain variances proactively
   - Don't wait for budget reviews

3. **Manage to Budget**
   - Treat budget as commitment, not suggestion
   - Get approval before exceeding budget
   - Find offsets within budget when possible

4. **Track Against Forecast**
   - Update forecast as new information emerges
   - Distinguish from budget (budget = plan, forecast = expected)
   - Use forecast for decision-making

5. **Learn and Improve**
   - Analyze accuracy at year-end
   - Identify what caused variances
   - Apply learnings to next budget cycle

## Common Budget Challenges

### Challenge: Unexpected Costs
**Solution:**
- Build in contingency (5-10% of budget)
- Maintain flexibility in discretionary spending
- Reallocate from lower priorities
- Submit in-year budget request if necessary

### Challenge: Revenue Shortfall Impact
**Solution:**
- Identify non-essential expenses to cut
- Delay discretionary projects
- Renegotiate contracts where possible
- Freeze hiring and travel if severe

### Challenge: Competing Priorities
**Solution:**
- Use prioritization framework (strategic alignment, ROI, urgency)
- Make trade-off decisions explicit
- Document decisions and rationale
- Revisit quarterly as conditions change

### Challenge: Lack of Historical Data
**Solution:**
- Research industry benchmarks
- Get quotes from vendors
- Pilot programs for better estimates
- Buffer estimates for uncertainty

### Challenge: Scope Creep on Projects
**Solution:**
- Define project scope clearly upfront
- Implement change control process
- Track scope changes and budget impacts
- Communicate trade-offs to stakeholders

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I need to spend more than budgeted?**
A: Submit an in-year budget request with justification. If urgent, discuss with your finance partner for possible interim approval.

**Q: Can I move money between budget line items?**
A: Within your department, yes, with manager approval. Between departments requires CFO approval. Submit reallocation request in budget system.

**Q: What happens to unused budget at year-end?**
A: Operating budget does not carry over. Capital project balances may carry forward with approval. Use-it-or-lose-it does not apply; don't waste budget.

**Q: How do I request a new position?**
A: Submit headcount request form with business case. Must be approved before posting role. Allow 6 weeks for approval and recruiting.

**Q: What's the difference between budget and forecast?**
A: Budget is the annual plan (doesn't change). Forecast is updated prediction of actuals (changes quarterly based on new information).

**Q: Can I get a budget increase mid-year?**
A: Yes, through in-year budget request process. Requires strong business case and appropriate approvals based on amount.

**Q: Who can approve my budget requests?**
A: Depends on amount. See approval thresholds section. Generally: small requests ($5K-$25K) by director, larger requests by VP or CFO.

**Q: How do I explain variances in monthly reviews?**
A: Provide specific explanation of what drove difference from budget. Include if temporary or ongoing, and any corrective actions.
