# Corporate Credit Card Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Version:** 3.1  
**Policy Owner:** Finance Department

## Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the issuance and use of corporate credit cards to facilitate business purchases while ensuring appropriate controls and compliance.

## Eligibility & Application

### Who Qualifies
**Automatic Eligibility:**
- Managers and above
- Frequent travelers (5+ trips per year)
- Employees with regular purchasing needs
- Remote workers requiring office supplies

**By Request:**
- Individual contributors with demonstrated need
- Project managers handling project expenses
- Administrative staff managing department purchases

### Application Process
1. Complete credit card application form at finance.company.com/card-request
2. Obtain manager approval with business justification
3. Finance reviews and approves/denies
4. Card issued and mailed to office address (7-10 business days)
5. Activate card and complete mandatory training
6. Sign cardholder agreement

### Card Limits
**Standard Limits:**
- Individual Contributor: $2,500/month
- Manager: $5,000/month
- Senior Manager: $10,000/month
- Director: $15,000/month
- VP: $25,000/month
- C-Suite: $50,000/month

**Limit Increases:**
- Temporary increase for specific event/purchase: Manager approval
- Permanent increase: VP + Finance approval
- Request at least 5 business days in advance

## Appropriate Uses

### Approved Business Expenses

**Travel-Related:**
- Airfare, hotels, rental cars
- Ground transportation (rideshare, taxis, parking)
- Business meals during travel
- Baggage fees and travel necessities

**Operating Expenses:**
- Office supplies and equipment
- Software subscriptions and licenses
- Books and professional publications
- Shipping and courier services

**Business Development:**
- Client meals and entertainment
- Conference registration and materials
- Networking events
- Business gifts (within policy limits)

**Professional Development:**
- Training course fees
- Professional membership dues
- Certification exam fees
- Educational materials

**Department Expenses:**
- Team meals and events
- Departmental supplies
- Marketing materials
- Vendor payments (when no PO)

### Strictly Prohibited Uses

**Personal Expenses:**
- Personal meals, groceries, household items
- Personal entertainment or subscriptions
- Family member expenses
- Personal travel or vacation costs
- Personal gifts or donations
- Cash advances or ATM withdrawals (except international travel)

**Other Prohibited:**
- Splitting purchases to circumvent approval limits
- Purchasing for other employees (except team events)
- Alcohol purchases except with meals (1 drink limit)
- Gift cards or prepaid cards
- Cryptocurrency or speculative investments
- Adult entertainment or inappropriate content
- Political contributions

**Consequences:**
- Immediate repayment of personal charges
- Card suspension or revocation
- Disciplinary action
- Termination for serious violations

## Purchase Procedures

### Before Making Purchase
1. **Verify it's a business expense** - Would company reimburse if paid personally?
2. **Check budget availability** - Ensure department has budget
3. **Get pre-approval if required** - Over $1,000 or unusual expense
4. **Confirm vendor accepts credit cards** - Some may require PO

### Making the Purchase
- Use card for business purposes only
- Obtain itemized receipt (not just card slip)
- Note business purpose if not obvious
- Keep digital or physical copy of receipt
- Report lost/stolen card immediately

### After Purchase
1. **Retain Receipt:** All receipts required, even under $25
2. **Upload Receipt:** Within 5 business days to expense system
3. **Add Details:** Business purpose, attendees (if meal), project code
4. **Review Monthly:** Check statement for accuracy
5. **Reconcile:** By 5th of following month
6. **Manager Approval:** Required before payment processing

## Receipt Requirements

### What's Required
- **All purchases:** Itemized receipt showing what was purchased
- **Meals:** Names of attendees and business purpose
- **Entertainment:** Who attended and business discussed
- **Travel:** Dates and destination
- **Software:** License details and users

### Receipt Formats Accepted
- Original paper receipts (scanned or photo)
- Email receipts (forwarded to receipts@company.com)
- PDF invoices
- Digital wallet receipts (with transaction detail)

### Missing Receipts
- **Lost Receipt Affidavit** required within 10 days
- Maximum $25 per item, $100 per statement without receipts
- Pattern of missing receipts may result in card suspension
- Charges without receipts after deadline may be treated as personal

### Retention
- Finance retains copies for 7 years
- Cardholder should keep copies during employment
- May be requested during audits or expense reviews

## Reconciliation & Payment

### Monthly Statement Cycle
- **Statement Close:** Last day of month
- **Statement Available:** 3rd business day of following month
- **Receipts Due:** 5th business day of following month
- **Reconciliation Due:** 10th business day of following month
- **Manager Approval Due:** 15th business day of following month
- **Payment:** 20th business day of following month

### Reconciliation Process
1. **Log in to expense system:** expenses.company.com
2. **Review transactions:** Statement automatically imported
3. **Match receipts:** Upload and link to each transaction
4. **Categorize expenses:** Select appropriate expense category and cost center
5. **Add business purpose:** Required for all transactions
6. **Submit for approval:** Send to manager when complete
7. **Respond to questions:** Answer any manager queries promptly

### Payment Responsibility
- **Company Pays:** All approved business expenses
- **Employee Pays:** Personal charges, missing receipts after deadline, policy violations
- **Finance processes payment** from company account to credit card company
- **Cardholder is NOT personally responsible** for payment if properly reconciled and approved

### Late Reconciliation
- **First Occurrence:** Reminder email from Finance
- **Second Occurrence:** Escalation to manager
- **Third Occurrence:** Card suspended until reconciled
- **Chronic Issues:** Card may be revoked

## Travel Expenses

### Airfare
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible
- Use corporate travel tool for best rates: travel.company.com
- Economy class for domestic flights under 3 hours
- Premium economy for longer flights (manager approval)
- Save boarding passes and receipts

### Hotels
- Book through corporate tool for negotiated rates
- Stay within daily rate limits (see Travel Policy)
- No mini-bar, movies, or personal services
- Resort fees and parking reimbursable if unavoidable
- Check out on time to avoid extra night charges

### Meals While Traveling
- Reasonable restaurant choices
- Daily limits: Breakfast $15, Lunch $25, Dinner $50
- Itemized receipts required over $25
- One alcoholic beverage per meal acceptable ($12 limit)
- Tips 15-20% on pre-tax amount

### Ground Transportation
- Rideshare preferred (Uber/Lyft standard service)
- Taxis when rideshare unavailable
- Rental cars only when necessary (mid-size or smaller)
- Personal car mileage reimbursed separately (not on card)
- Keep all receipts and note destination

## Client Entertainment

### Guidelines
- Must have legitimate business purpose
- Appropriate venue and cost for relationship
- At least one client/customer must attend
- Reasonable food and beverage
- Alcohol limited to appropriate amount for occasion

### Documentation Required
- Itemized receipt
- Names and companies of all attendees
- Business purpose and topics discussed
- Anticipated business outcome

### Limits
- **Manager:** Up to $500 without pre-approval
- **Director:** Up to $2,000 without pre-approval
- **VP:** Up to $10,000 without pre-approval
- **Higher amounts:** CFO pre-approval required

### Prohibited
- Lavish or excessive entertainment
- Activities that could be seen as bribes
- Entertainment of government officials (requires legal approval)
- Adult entertainment venues
- Spouses/family unless business purpose

## Software & Subscriptions

### Allowed
- Business tools and applications
- Professional subscriptions (LinkedIn, industry publications)
- Domain registrations and web hosting
- Cloud storage for business use
- Project management and collaboration tools

### Requirements
- IT approval for all software purchases
- Security review for new vendors
- Must be used for business purposes
- Document users and business need
- Coordinate with IT to avoid duplicate purchases

### Recurring Subscriptions
- Set calendar reminder for renewal dates
- Cancel unused subscriptions promptly
- Review annually for continued need
- IT maintains master list of all subscriptions

## International Use

### Pre-Travel Setup
- Notify card company of travel dates and countries: (888) XXX-XXXX
- No foreign transaction fees on corporate cards
- Verify card will work in destination country
- Bring backup payment method
- Know how to report lost/stolen card internationally

### Currency & Conversions
- Charges automatically converted to USD
- Conversion rate set by Visa/Mastercard on transaction date
- No need to manually convert for reconciliation
- Avoid dynamic currency conversion (decline when offered)

### Cash Advances
- Generally prohibited
- Allowed only when necessary in international locations
- Limit $500 per trip
- Requires pre-approval from manager
- Repay unused cash within 5 days of return
- Subject to cash advance fees and interest

## Security & Fraud Prevention

### Protect Your Card
- Never share card number or security code
- Don't store card details on unsecure websites
- Use secure websites only (https://)
- Don't let card out of your sight during transactions
- Shred receipts with full card numbers

### Suspicious Activity
- Monitor transactions regularly
- Report unauthorized charges immediately
- Watch for small test charges (fraud indicator)
- Review statement for unfamiliar merchants

### Lost or Stolen Card
**Immediate Actions:**
1. Call card company 24/7 hotline: 1-888-XXX-XXXX
2. Report to Finance: cardfraud@company.com
3. Complete fraud affidavit if fraudulent charges
4. Request replacement card (arrives in 3-5 days)
5. Update any recurring charges to new card number

**Your Protection:**
- Zero liability for fraudulent charges
- Company absorbs cost of fraud if reported promptly
- Delay in reporting may result in cardholder liability

### Data Breaches
- Notify Finance if merchant announces breach
- Monitor statement for 6 months after breach
- Card may be reissued proactively
- Company handles breach response

## Card Termination

### When Employment Ends
- Card must be returned to HR on last day
- Cut card in half if returning by mail
- Final expense report due within 10 days
- Any unreported charges billed to final paycheck
- Unpaid personal charges deducted from final pay

### Voluntary Surrender
- May return card if no longer needed
- Email Finance 2 weeks in advance
- Complete final reconciliation
- No penalty for voluntary return

### Involuntary Revocation
**Reasons:**
- Policy violations
- Late reconciliation (chronic)
- Personal use violations
- Fraud or misuse
- Terminated employment
- Position change

**Process:**
- Finance notifies cardholder and manager
- Card deactivated immediately
- Must return physical card
- May affect performance review
- May be eligible for reinstatement after 6 months

## Audits & Compliance

### Transaction Monitoring
- All transactions reviewed by Finance
- Automated alerts for unusual activity
- Random monthly audits of expenses
- Annual comprehensive review per cardholder

### Red Flags
- Purchases just under approval limits
- High-risk merchant categories
- Weekend or late-night purchases
- Geographic anomalies
- Frequent purchases from same vendor
- Missing receipts pattern

### Audit Process
**If Selected for Audit:**
1. Notification from Finance or Internal Audit
2. Provide additional documentation as requested
3. May include sample of transactions or full year review
4. Results shared with cardholder and manager
5. Action plan for any issues identified

### Consequences of Non-Compliance
**Minor Issues:**
- Coaching and retraining
- Increased monitoring
- Temporary card suspension

**Serious Violations:**
- Card revocation
- Repayment of misused funds
- Written warning
- Performance review impact

**Severe Violations:**
- Termination of employment
- Legal action for fraud
- Criminal charges if warranted
- Repayment plus penalties

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use my corporate card for personal emergencies?**
A: No. Corporate cards are for business use only. Personal charges must be repaid immediately.

**Q: What if I accidentally make a personal purchase?**
A: Note it on your expense report as personal. Finance will bill you for repayment. One-time mistakes are understandable; patterns are not.

**Q: Do I need approval for every purchase?**
A: Purchases within policy and under $1,000 don't require pre-approval. Unusual or large purchases should be approved in advance.

**Q: What if a merchant doesn't accept credit cards?**
A: Pay personally and submit expense report for reimbursement, or request check payment from AP.

**Q: Can I earn personal rewards on my corporate card?**
A: No. Corporate cards don't earn personal points/miles. Business benefit of rebates goes to company.

**Q: What if my manager is slow to approve?**
A: Send reminder email. If no response in 3 days, escalate to manager's manager and CC Finance.

**Q: Can I add my corporate card to Apple Pay/Google Pay?**
A: Yes, digital wallets are acceptable. Still need itemized receipts showing what was purchased.

**Q: What if the vendor doesn't provide itemized receipts?**
A: Request itemized invoice. If unavailable, explain in expense system. Pattern may require alternative payment method.

**Q: Can I use my card for employee gifts or recognition?**
A: Yes, up to $50 per person for work anniversaries, birthdays, etc. Document recipient and occasion.

**Q: What happens if I go over my card limit?**
A: Transaction will decline. Request temporary increase in advance or use alternative payment method.

- Report suspicious activity or fraud immediately

- Accept disciplinary action for policy violations
