# Travel & Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Chief Financial Officer  
**Version:** 4.3

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for business travel expenses and reimbursement procedures to ensure appropriate use of company funds while meeting business objectives.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all employees traveling for company business, including:
- Client meetings and site visits
- Conferences and training events
- Internal meetings requiring travel
- Recruiting and interview travel

## 3. Travel Authorization

### Pre-Approval Required
All travel must be approved BEFORE booking:
- **Domestic travel:** Manager approval
- **International travel:** Manager + VP approval
- **Conference attendance:** Manager approval + registration confirmation
- **Non-standard travel:** CFO approval

### Authorization Process
1. Submit travel request in expense system 2 weeks in advance
2. Include business justification and estimated costs
3. Await approval before making reservations
4. Book through preferred vendors when possible
5. Save all receipts and documentation

### Emergency Travel
- Verbal approval from manager acceptable
- Submit written request within 24 hours
- Provide explanation of emergency circumstances

## 4. Air Travel

### Booking Requirements
- **Advance Booking:** Book at least 14 days in advance when possible
- **Preferred Vendor:** Use corporate travel agency (TravelPro) for best rates
- **Online Booking Tool:** travel.company.com
- **Direct Flights:** Book direct flights when cost difference is less than $100

### Class of Service
**Domestic Flights:**
- Trips under 3 hours: Economy class only
- Trips over 3 hours: Economy Plus/Comfort+ allowed
- Executive level (VP+): Business class at discretion

**International Flights:**
- Flights under 6 hours: Economy Plus/Premium Economy
- Flights 6-10 hours: Premium Economy or Business class
- Flights over 10 hours: Business class allowed
- C-suite: Business/First class at discretion

### Additional Guidelines
- **Baggage Fees:** One checked bag reimbursable for trips over 2 days
- **Seat Selection:** Basic seat selection included; premium seats require justification
- **Flight Changes:** Change fees reimbursable if business-related
- **Cancellations:** Cancel unused tickets promptly for credit
- **Travel Insurance:** Optional for international travel (company-paid)

### Non-Reimbursable
- Upgrades using personal funds
- Frequent flyer program fees
- Airport club memberships (unless executive level)
- Premium seat selection without justification

## 5. Ground Transportation

### Rental Cars
**When Approved:**
- Multiple meeting locations requiring vehicle
- Limited public transportation at destination
- Group travel (3+ people)
- Cost-effective compared to rideshare for trip duration

**Requirements:**
- Mid-size or smaller vehicle (SUV if 4+ passengers)
- Book through corporate vendor (Enterprise, Hertz)
- Decline rental car insurance (covered by company policy)
- Fuel: Return with full tank to avoid premium charges
- Tolls and parking reimbursable with receipts

### Rideshare & Taxis
- **Preferred:** Uber, Lyft for airport transfers and local travel
- **Standard Service:** UberX, Lyft Standard (not luxury options)
- **Taxis:** Reimbursable when rideshare unavailable
- **Receipts:** Required for all rides over $25
- **Tips:** 15-20% acceptable

### Personal Vehicle Mileage
- **Rate:** $0.655 per mile (current IRS rate)
- **Documentation:** Submit start/end addresses and odometer readings
- **Calculation:** Use Google Maps for mileage verification
- **Parking & Tolls:** Reimbursable with receipts
- **Insurance:** Personal auto insurance required

### Public Transportation
- Trains, subways, buses fully reimbursable
- Purchase day/week passes when cost-effective
- Retain tickets or provide transaction screenshots

### Airport Parking
- **Short trips (1-3 days):** Economy parking acceptable
- **Longer trips (4+ days):** Airport parking or off-site economy lot
- **Alternative:** Rideshare may be more cost-effective; compare costs
- **Valet parking:** Not reimbursable unless medically necessary

## 6. Lodging

### Hotel Selection
- **Preferred Hotels:** Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt (corporate rates available)
- **Booking:** Use corporate travel tool for negotiated rates
- **Location:** Stay near meeting location to minimize transportation costs
- **Alternative:** Airbnb/VRBO acceptable for extended stays (5+ nights)

### Rate Limits
**Domestic:**
- Major cities (NYC, SF, LA, Chicago): $250/night
- Mid-size cities: $175/night
- Small cities/suburbs: $125/night
- Conferences: Conference hotel rate acceptable even if above limits

**International:**
- Tier 1 cities (London, Tokyo, Paris): $300/night
- Tier 2 cities: $200/night
- Other locations: $150/night

### Extended Stay
- Stays over 7 nights: Consider extended stay hotels or corporate housing
- Weekly rates often more economical
- Kitchen facilities reduce meal costs

### Lodging Guidelines
- **Check-in/out:** Standard dates to minimize extra nights
- **Room Type:** Standard room; suites require VP approval
- **Incidentals:** Personal items not reimbursable
- **Resort Fees:** Reimbursable if unavoidable
- **Early Check-in/Late Checkout:** Fees not reimbursable
- **Mini-bar:** Not reimbursable
- **Movies/Entertainment:** Not reimbursable
- **Hotel Internet:** Reimbursable if needed for work

## 7. Meals & Entertainment

### Per Diem Rates (Daily Limits)
**Domestic:**
- Breakfast: $15
- Lunch: $25
- Dinner: $50
- Total daily: $90

**International:**
- Use GSA rates for specific countries
- Generally 25% higher than domestic rates

### Meal Guidelines
- **Actual Cost:** Submit actual meal costs with itemized receipts
- **Receipts Required:** All meals over $25
- **Alcohol:** One drink per meal reimbursable ($12 limit)
- **Tips:** 15-20% on pre-tax amount
- **Solo Dining:** Order reasonably; avoid expensive restaurants
- **Group Meals:** Pre-approval required for 4+ people

### Business Meals
**Client/Customer Entertainment:**
- Pre-approval required for meals over $100/person
- Itemized receipt required
- Document attendees and business purpose
- Reasonable alcohol for clients acceptable
- Select appropriate venue for business discussion

**Team Meals:**
- Manager may approve team dinners during travel
- Reasonable cost per person
- Alternative to individual meals, not in addition to
- Builds team relationships and morale

### Non-Reimbursable
- Alcohol beyond one drink per meal
- Room service (unless only option available)
- Excessive or luxury dining
- Meals with spouse/family (unless business purpose)
- Snacks and beverages from hotel mini-bar

## 8. Conference & Training

### Registration Fees
- Early bird rates required when booking in advance
- Conference materials and sessions included
- Supplemental workshops require separate approval

### Conference Expenses
**Reimbursable:**
- Registration fees
- Conference hotel (even if above daily rate)
- Meals not provided by conference
- Conference materials and books
- Networking events (with receipt)

**Not Reimbursable:**
- Spouse/guest registration or travel
- Tourist activities
- Non-business social events

## 9. Other Reimbursable Expenses

### Communication
- Mobile phone charges for business calls
- International roaming (activate company plan)
- WiFi charges when hotel WiFi inadequate for work

### Business Services
- Printing and shipping business documents
- Business center services at hotel
- Currency exchange fees (reasonable)

### Miscellaneous
- Passport and visa fees for business travel
- TSA PreCheck/Global Entry ($85 every 5 years)
- Required vaccinations for international travel
- Baggage fees (one checked bag for 3+ day trips)

## 10. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

### Personal Items
- Clothing and personal care items
- Toiletries and medications
- Gym fees and personal fitness
- Laundry for trips under 5 days
- Haircuts and personal services

### Entertainment
- In-flight entertainment
- Hotel movies and games
- Personal sightseeing and tours
- Sporting events and concerts
- Spa services

### Other
- Traffic violations and parking tickets
- Lost luggage (file claim with airline)
- Personal travel insurance
- Excess baggage fees
- Pet care during travel
- Upgrades using personal preference

## 11. Expense Reporting

### Submission Timeline
- **Deadline:** Submit expenses within 30 days of trip completion
- **Late Submission:** Expenses over 60 days may be denied
- **Monthly Travel:** Submit at month end

### Required Documentation
- **Receipts:** Required for all expenses over $25
- **Itemized Receipts:** Required for meals, hotels, rental cars
- **Credit Card Statements:** Not acceptable as sole documentation
- **Missing Receipts:** Affidavit required; limit $25 per item, $100 per trip

### Expense Report Format
1. **Trip Information:** Dates, destination, business purpose
2. **Expense Details:** Date, vendor, amount, category, business purpose
3. **Attendees:** List for business meals
4. **Receipts:** Attach all supporting documentation
5. **Mileage:** Provide start/end locations
6. **Approvals:** Manager approval before submission

### Submission Process
1. Log in to expense system: expenses.company.com
2. Create new expense report
3. Add expenses with receipts
4. Categorize each expense correctly
5. Provide business justification
6. Submit to manager for approval
7. Finance reviews and processes
8. Reimbursement via direct deposit (2 weeks)

### Corporate Credit Cards
- Available to frequent travelers
- Monthly reconciliation required
- Personal charges prohibited
- Report lost/stolen cards immediately
- Charges due even if expense denied

## 12. International Travel

### Pre-Travel Requirements
- Passport valid for 6+ months beyond travel dates
- Obtain necessary visas (company reimburses fees)
- Register travel with company security
- Review country-specific travel advisories
- Enroll in international phone plan
- Obtain international health insurance (company-provided)

### Currency & Payments
- Use corporate credit card when possible (best exchange rates)
- Limit cash advances to necessary amounts
- Retain currency exchange receipts
- Convert expenses to USD using rate on transaction date
- Use Oanda.com or expense system for conversion rates

### International Expense Limits
- Follow host country GSA rates for meals
- Hotel rates typically higher; use judgment
- Tipping customs vary; research local practices
- VAT/GST often reimbursable; retain tax receipts

## 13. Compliance & Audit

### Policy Violations
- **Minor Violations:** Manager counseling and correction
- **Pattern of Violations:** Written warning and training
- **Serious Violations:** Reimbursement denial, potential termination
- **Fraud:** Immediate termination and legal action

### Audit Process
- Random monthly audits of expense reports
- Annual comprehensive audit by department
- Anomaly detection by finance system
- Manager review before approval

### Consequences of Non-Compliance
- Expense denial and personal liability
- Loss of corporate card privileges
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Legal action for fraudulent expenses

## 14. Special Circumstances

### Extended Business Travel
**Trips over 30 days:**
- Weekly calls home reimbursable (30 minutes)
- Laundry and dry cleaning reimbursable
- One trip home per month (if cost-effective)
- Temporary housing allowance available

### Relocations
- Separate relocation policy applies
- Coordinate with HR for relocation benefits
- This policy does not cover relocation expenses

### Recruitment Travel
**Candidate Travel:**
- Company pays for candidate travel to interviews
- Standard policy rates apply
- Arrange through recruiting coordinator

**Employee Recruiting Travel:**
- Standard travel policy applies
- Campus recruiting trips pre-approved by HR

### Spouse/Family Travel
- Not reimbursable unless business necessity
- CFO approval required for exceptions
- Employee pays incremental costs (larger room, extra meal)
- Additional baggage fees not reimbursable
