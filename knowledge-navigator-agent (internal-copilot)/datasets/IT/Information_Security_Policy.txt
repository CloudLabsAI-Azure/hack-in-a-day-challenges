# Information Security Policy

**Version 3.0 | Effective January 2024**

## Purpose
Protect company information assets, ensure data confidentiality, integrity, and availability, and comply with regulatory requirements.

## Scope
All employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties with access to company systems or data.

## Password Requirements

### Strength
- Minimum 12 characters
- Must include: uppercase, lowercase, number, special character
- No dictionary words or common patterns
- Cannot reuse last 5 passwords
- Different passwords for different systems

### Management
- Change every 90 days
- Immediate change if compromised
- Use company password manager: 1Password
- Never share or write down passwords
- No storing in browsers or unencrypted files

### Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Required for all business applications
- Use Microsoft Authenticator or hardware token
- Backup codes stored securely
- Report lost MFA device immediately

## Data Classification

### Public
- Marketing materials, press releases
- Published content
- No special controls required

### Internal
- General business information
- Employee directory (basic info)
- Internal policies and procedures
- Access: Authenticated employees only

### Confidential
- Financial data, strategic plans
- Customer lists, contracts
- Employee personal information
- Access: Need-to-know basis, manager approval

### Restricted
- Trade secrets, source code
- M&A information, legal matters
- Regulated data (PCI, HIPAA, PII)
- Access: Executive approval, mandatory training

## Data Handling

### Storage
- Company data only on approved systems
- Use OneDrive/SharePoint for file storage
- No personal cloud storage (Dropbox personal, Google Drive personal)
- Encrypt confidential/restricted data at rest
- USB drives encrypted or prohibited

### Transmission
- Email encryption for confidential data
- Secure file transfer for restricted data
- No sensitive data via SMS or personal messaging
- Use company-approved sharing methods

### Disposal
- Shred paper documents with sensitive data
- Wipe hard drives before disposal (IT does this)
- Delete data when no longer needed
- Secure erase for confidential/restricted data

## Acceptable Use Policy

### Approved Uses
- Business communications and collaboration
- Job-related research and learning
- Reasonable personal use during breaks
- Professional social media for business purposes

### Prohibited Uses
- Illegal activities or copyright violations
- Accessing/distributing inappropriate content
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Installing unlicensed or unapproved software
- Cryptocurrency mining
- Personal business ventures
- Excessive personal use impacting productivity

### Email & Communication
- Professional tone in business communications
- No harassment, discrimination, or offensive content
- Assume emails may be monitored or disclosed
- Use work email for work, not personal business
- Be cautious with "Reply All"

### Internet & Web
- Access appropriate business and personal sites
- No streaming video/music excessively
- No gambling or adult content sites
- Social media okay during breaks
- Be mindful of bandwidth usage

## Device Security

### Company-Issued Devices
- Laptop/desktop, phone, tablet
- Full disk encryption enabled
- Antivirus/endpoint protection installed
- Auto-lock after 5 minutes inactive
- Keep OS and applications updated
- Report lost/stolen immediately: (555) 123-4100

### BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)
- Must enroll in Mobile Device Management (MDM)
- Company can wipe corporate data remotely
- Separate work and personal data
- Meet minimum security requirements
- Not recommended for confidential data

### Physical Security
- Lock computer when away from desk (Win+L or Ctrl+Cmd+Q)
- Don't leave devices unattended in public
- Cable lock for laptops in office
- Keep devices in sight while traveling
- Report theft to police and IT immediately

## Remote Work Security

### Home Network
- Secure WiFi with WPA2/WPA3 encryption
- Change default router password
- Keep router firmware updated
- Separate network for smart devices if possible

### Public WiFi
- Always use VPN on public networks
- No sensitive work without VPN
- Verify network name with venue
- Beware of fake hotspots
- Use phone hotspot for sensitive work

### VPN
- Required for accessing company network remotely
- Auto-connects for company apps
- Contact IT if VPN issues: (555) 123-4000
- Do not disable VPN while working

### Work Environment
- Privacy for confidential calls/video meetings
- Screen not visible to family/visitors
- Secure workspace for confidential documents
- Lock door when handling restricted data

## Phishing & Social Engineering

### Warning Signs
- Urgent requests for action
- Requests for credentials or payment
- Suspicious sender or reply-to address
- Generic greetings ("Dear Customer")
- Spelling and grammar errors
- Unexpected attachments or links
- Too-good-to-be-true offers

### What To Do
- Hover over links before clicking (check actual URL)
- Verify sender by calling known phone number
- Don't download unexpected attachments
- Report suspicious emails: Click "Report Phishing" button or forward to phishing@company.com
- When in doubt, call IT: (555) 123-4000

### Never Share
- Passwords or MFA codes
- Social Security or bank details via email
- Credit card numbers in email
- Answers to security questions

## Incident Response

### Security Incidents (Report Immediately)
- Suspected malware or ransomware
- Lost or stolen device
- Phishing email clicked / credentials entered
- Unauthorized access to account
- Data breach or leak
- Suspicious activity on systems

### Reporting
**Security Team:**
- Phone: (555) 123-4100 (24/7)
- Email: security@company.com
- After-hours emergency: (555) 123-9999

**What to Report:**
- What happened and when
- What systems/data affected
- Actions you've taken
- Your contact information

**Do NOT:**
- Pay ransom (contact security first)
- Try to "fix it" yourself
- Delete evidence
- Wait to report
- Share incident details publicly

## Compliance & Training

### Annual Requirements
- Security awareness training (required)
- Phishing simulation tests (quarterly)
- Policy acknowledgment (annual)
- Role-specific training as assigned

### Audits & Monitoring
- IT may monitor systems for security
- Random audits of access and usage
- Logs retained for security investigations
- Compliance with SOC 2, GDPR, CCPA

### Violations & Consequences
**Minor:** Warning, retraining
**Moderate:** Written warning, access restrictions
**Severe:** Termination, legal action
