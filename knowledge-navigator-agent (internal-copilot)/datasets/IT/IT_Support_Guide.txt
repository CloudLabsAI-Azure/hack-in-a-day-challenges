## Ticket Priority Levels

### Priority 1 - Critical (Response: 15 minutes)
**Complete business stoppage affecting multiple users**

Examples:
- Network outage affecting entire office
- Email system down company-wide
- Critical business application unavailable
- Data breach or security incident
- Ransomware or malware outbreak
- Complete phone system failure

Actions:
- Major incident declared
- All-hands IT response
- Executive notification
- Regular status updates every 30 minutes

### Priority 2 - High (Response: 1 hour)
**Significant impact to business operations or individual completely unable to work**

Examples:
- Individual cannot access email
- Laptop completely non-functional
- Cannot access critical application for job
- VPN not working (remote employee)
- Shared printer down (department impact)
- Database slow/timing out

Actions:
- Assigned to senior technician
- Workaround provided if possible
- Management notified if not resolved in 4 hours

### Priority 3 - Medium (Response: 4 hours)
**Moderate impact, workaround available, or non-critical function**

Examples:
- Software not working as expected
- Slow computer performance
- Minor email issues (attachments, formatting)
- Non-critical application access
- Desk phone issues (can use mobile)
- Printer issues (other printer available)

Actions:
- Assigned during business hours
- Resolution targeted within 1-2 business days
- Status updated daily

### Priority 4 - Low (Response: 1 business day)
**Minimal impact, informational, or future request**

Examples:
- How-to questions
- Feature requests
- Software training request
- Equipment upgrade request (cosmetic)
- Report generation assistance
- Nice-to-have improvements

Actions:
- Assigned as time permits
- Batched with similar requests
- Resolution within 5 business days
- May be addressed in group training

## Creating Effective Tickets

### Required Information

**For All Tickets:**
- Your name, email, phone, department
- Brief descriptive subject line
- Detailed description of issue
- When did it start?
- Does it happen consistently or intermittently?
- Error messages (exact text or screenshot)
- What were you trying to do?
- What did you expect to happen?
- What actually happened?

**For Hardware Issues:**
- Device type (laptop, desktop, phone, printer)
- Asset tag number (sticker on device)
- Model information
- Is device under warranty?
- Has it been dropped or damaged?

**For Software Issues:**
- Application name and version
- Operating system (Windows, Mac, iOS, Android)
- Browser name and version (if web-based)
- Screenshot of error message
- Steps to reproduce issue
- Does it happen to others or just you?

**For Access Issues:**
- What system/application?
- Your username
- What access do you need?
- Business justification
- How long do you need access?
- Who else has similar access?

### Helpful Details to Include

**Do:**
- Be specific and detailed
- Include screenshots (use Snipping Tool or Snip & Sketch)
- List troubleshooting already attempted
- Note if issue is urgent with reason
- Provide callback number
- Mention if multiple people affected
- Note workaround if you found one

**Don't:**
- Write "urgent" unless truly critical
- Say "it doesn't work" without details
- Leave out contact information
- Send multiple tickets for same issue
- Include sensitive data like passwords
- Demand specific technician
- Use all caps or excessive punctuation

### Screenshot Best Practices

**What to Capture:**
- Full error message with error code
- Entire screen showing context
- URL in browser address bar
- System tray showing network/VPN status
- Application version (usually in Help > About)

**How to Take Screenshots:**
- Windows: Win+Shift+S (Snipping Tool)
- Mac: Cmd+Shift+4
- Both: Use phone camera for screen crashes

**Attach to Ticket:**
- Upload to ticket portal
- Email to helpdesk@company.com with ticket number in subject
- Paste inline if using web form

## Ticket Lifecycle

### 1. Ticket Submission
- You submit ticket via portal, email, or phone
- Automated confirmation sent with ticket number
- Ticket enters queue

### 2. Initial Triage (within 30 minutes)
- Help desk reviews and categorizes
- Priority assigned
- Ticket routed to appropriate team
- May request additional information

### 3. Assignment
- Assigned to available technician based on skillset
- Assignment notification sent to you
- Technician may contact you for clarification

### 4. Investigation
- Technician researches and troubleshoots
- May request remote access
- Tests solutions in lab environment
- Consults vendor or senior staff if needed

### 5. Resolution
- Solution implemented
- Technician updates ticket with resolution
- You receive notification
- Asked to confirm issue resolved

### 6. Closure
- You confirm resolution (or request more help)
- Ticket marked resolved
- Satisfaction survey sent
- Ticket closed after 3 days if no response

### Throughout Process
- Receive email updates when status changes
- Can check ticket status anytime at helpdesk.company.com
- Can add comments or information to ticket
- Can request technician callback

## Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

### Response Times
| Priority | Initial Response | Resolution Target |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Critical (P1) | 15 minutes | 4 hours |
| High (P2) | 1 hour | 8 hours |
| Medium (P3) | 4 hours | 2 business days |
| Low (P4) | 1 business day | 5 business days |

### Resolution Time
- Clock starts when ticket submitted
- Paused when waiting for your response
- Paused during off-hours for non-emergency

- Workaround provided if possible

## Tips for Faster Resolution

**Before Submitting Ticket:**
- Restart computer (fixes 30% of issues)
- Check cables and connections
- Verify internet connection
- Try different browser
- Check if others have same issue
- Search knowledge base

**In Your Ticket:**
- Use descriptive subject line
- Include all relevant details

- Note urgency and business impact
- Provide callback number
- Mention troubleshooting attempted

**During Resolution:**

## FAQs

**Q: How do I check my ticket status?**
A: Log in to helpdesk.company.com and click "My Tickets" or check your email for updates.

**Q: Can I request a specific technician?**
A: Tickets are assigned based on availability and expertise. You can request specific technician in comments but cannot guarantee.

**Q: What if I need help outside business hours?**
A: Phone support available 24/7 for emergencies. Submit ticket online anytime; response during business hours unless emergency.

**Q: How long do tickets stay open?**
A: Active tickets stay open until resolved. If waiting your response over 5 days, ticket may be closed (can reopen if needed).

**Q: Can I submit tickets for someone else?**
A: Yes, but include their name, email, and contact info. They will be primary contact for ticket.

**Q: What if my issue returns after ticket closed?**
A: Reopen existing ticket or reference it in new ticket. Helps track related issues.

**Q: Do you support personal devices?**
A: Limited support for personal devices accessing company resources (email on phone). No support for personal use issues.

**Q: How do I provide feedback on IT support?**
A: Complete satisfaction survey after ticket closes or email feedback to itsupport-manager@company.com.
