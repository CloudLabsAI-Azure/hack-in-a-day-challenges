# Remote Work Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Version:** 2.1  
**Department:** Human Resources

## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy establishes guidelines for remote work arrangements to support flexible work options while maintaining productivity, collaboration, and company culture.

**Applies to:** All full-time employees who have completed probationary period (90 days)

## 2. Remote Work Options

### Hybrid Schedule
**Standard Arrangement:**
- Work remotely 2-3 days per week
- In-office required days: Tuesday and Thursday (team collaboration days)
- Flexible remote days: Monday, Wednesday, Friday
- Manager approval required for consistent schedule

**Eligibility:**
- Completed 90-day probation period
- Meets or exceeds performance expectations
- Role suitable for remote work
- Home workspace meets requirements

### Full-Time Remote
**Criteria:**
- Approved on case-by-case basis
- Must live within 2 hours of office for quarterly in-person meetings
- Role is 100% suitable for remote work
- Exceptional performance history
- Senior leadership approval required

**Requirements:**
- Quarterly in-person attendance for team meetings
- Available during core business hours (10 AM - 3 PM local time)
- Maintain company-provided equipment and security standards
- Dedicated home office space

## 3. Home Workspace Requirements

### Physical Space
- Dedicated workspace separate from personal living areas
- Adequate lighting and ergonomic setup
- Quiet environment conducive to work and video calls
- Professional background for video meetings

### Technology Requirements
- High-speed internet (minimum 25 Mbps download, 5 Mbps upload)
- Company-provided laptop and accessories
- Webcam and headset for meetings
- Secure WiFi network (encrypted, password protected)
- Backup internet option (mobile hotspot)

### Safety & Security
- Workspace free from hazards
- Secure storage for company equipment and confidential information
- Compliance with home insurance requirements
- Adherence to data protection protocols

## 4. Work Schedule & Availability

### Core Hours
- Available for meetings and collaboration: 10 AM - 3 PM (employee's local time zone)
- Respond to emails and messages within 2 hours during core hours
- Update calendar with availability and time off
- Set status in Teams/Slack (Available, In Meeting, Away, Do Not Disturb)

### Flexibility
- Start/end times flexible outside core hours
- Must complete standard 40-hour work week
- Coordinate schedule with team for coverage
- Communicate schedule changes in advance

### Time Zone Considerations
- Employees may work from different time zones with manager approval
- Must overlap at least 4 hours with team's primary time zone
- Responsible for attending meetings scheduled during overlap hours
- Notify team of time zone changes

## 5. Communication Expectations

### Responsiveness
- Check email and Teams/Slack multiple times during work hours
- Respond to manager requests within 2 hours
- Update status when away from desk
- Join video calls with camera on for team meetings

### Team Collaboration
- Attend all scheduled team meetings
- Participate actively in video calls
- Over-communicate progress and blockers
- Use collaboration tools effectively (Teams, SharePoint, project management software)

### Meeting Etiquette
- Join meetings on time
- Use professional background or blur background
- Mute when not speaking
- Be present and engaged (no multitasking)
- Dress professionally from waist up

## 6. Performance & Productivity

### Expectations
- Maintain same productivity standards as in-office work
- Meet all deadlines and deliverables
- Quality of work unchanged
- Active participation in team activities

### Monitoring
- Performance assessed on outcomes, not hours logged
- Regular check-ins with manager (weekly 1-on-1s)
- Project progress tracked through management tools
- Self-report weekly accomplishments

### Performance Issues
- Underperformance may result in modified remote arrangement
- Manager may require increased in-office presence
- Performance improvement plan may include return to office
- Continued issues may result in loss of remote privilege

## 7. Equipment & Expenses

### Company-Provided Equipment
- Laptop computer
- Monitor (if needed for role)
- Keyboard and mouse
- Webcam and headset
- Docking station
- Software licenses

### Home Office Stipend
- One-time $500 setup allowance for:
  - Desk and chair
  - Lighting
  - Office supplies
  - Cable management
- Submit receipts for reimbursement within 30 days

### Ongoing Expenses
- Internet subsidy: $50/month
- Mobile phone reimbursement: $50/month (if role requires)
- Electricity and utilities not reimbursed
- Home office tax deductions (consult personal tax advisor)

### Equipment Care
- Employee responsible for security and maintenance
- Report damage or issues to IT immediately
- Return all equipment upon separation
- No personal use of company equipment outside work hours

## 8. Security & Data Protection

### Information Security
- Use company VPN for all work activities
- Never access company systems on public WiFi without VPN
- Lock computer when stepping away
- Secure physical documents in locked storage
- Shred confidential documents

### Data Handling
- No downloading company data to personal devices
- Use company-approved cloud storage only (OneDrive, SharePoint)
- No sharing credentials with family members
- Report lost or stolen equipment immediately
- Enable multi-factor authentication on all systems

### Confidentiality
- Ensure privacy during confidential calls
- No visible company information to visitors
- Close blinds/doors for sensitive discussions
- Family members should not overhear confidential matters

## 9. In-Office Requirements

### Mandatory In-Office Days
- Team collaboration days: Every Tuesday and Thursday
- Department meetings: First Wednesday of month
- All-hands meetings: Last Thursday of quarter
- Special events: Company meetings, trainings, celebrations

### Office Access
- Badge access maintained for all employees
- Book workspace desk through office hoteling system
- Return to assigned desk/office if available
- Comply with in-office safety protocols

### Advance Notice for Office Presence
- Manager may require in-office attendance with 48-hour notice
- Annual in-person events announced with 30-day notice
- Client meetings may require in-office presence

## 10. Remote Work Request Process

### Initial Request
1. Discuss remote work desire with manager
2. Complete Remote Work Request Form
3. Manager evaluates role suitability and performance
4. HR reviews and approves/denies request
5. IT assesses technology needs
6. Complete remote work training module
7. Sign Remote Work Agreement

**Timeline:** 2-3 weeks from request to approval

### Trial Period
- First 30 days are trial period
- Manager evaluates performance and communication
- Employee assesses home workspace adequacy
- Either party may end arrangement during trial
- Full implementation after successful trial

### Annual Renewal
- Remote work arrangements reviewed annually
- Submit renewal request 30 days before expiration
- Manager assesses continued suitability
- Arrangement may be modified based on business needs

## 11. Modification or Termination

### Business Changes
Company reserves right to modify or terminate remote work arrangements due to:
- Business needs or restructuring
- Client requirements
- Team collaboration needs
- Technology limitations
- Office space changes

**Notice:** 30 days advance notice when possible

### Performance-Based Changes
- Performance decline may require increased office presence
- Communication issues may limit remote work
- Manager discretion to modify arrangement
- HR involvement in significant changes

### Employee-Initiated Changes
- Employee may request to reduce remote days
- Return to full-time office with 2 weeks notice
- Temporary changes for personal reasons (manager approval)

## 12. Travel & Work Location

### Travel While Remote
- Notify manager before working from location other than home
- Must maintain same schedule and availability
- Ensure reliable internet and workspace
- No international work without HR approval
- Travel expenses for personal travel not reimbursed

### Permanent Relocation
- Moving to new location requires HR approval
- Must remain within 2 hours of office
- Update address and emergency contact information
- Ensure new location meets workspace requirements
- Tax implications employee's responsibility

### "Work from Anywhere" Days
- 4 weeks per year to work from any location
- Must provide 2 weeks advance notice
- Manager approval required
- Must be available during core hours
- Same performance expectations apply

## 13. Compliance & Legal

### Workers' Compensation
- Work-related injuries at home covered by workers' compensation
- Report incidents to manager and HR immediately
- Home office safety guidelines must be followed
- Company may inspect home workspace for safety

### Tax Implications
- Employee responsible for understanding tax implications
- Consult personal tax advisor for home office deductions
- Company does not provide tax advice
- Working from other states may have tax consequences

### Employment Laws
- All company policies apply to remote work
- Labor laws of employee's work location apply
- Overtime rules apply same as in-office work
- Harassment and discrimination policies fully applicable

## 15. Best Practices for Remote Success

### Maintain Routine
- Set consistent work hours
- Create morning routine to transition to work mode
- Take regular breaks
- End workday at consistent time

### Stay Connected
- Over-communicate with team
- Schedule regular video check-ins
- Participate in virtual social events
- Build relationships with colleagues

### Manage Boundaries
- Separate work and personal life
- Communicate boundaries with household members
- Don't work from bed or couch
- Take lunch break away from desk

### Stay Productive
- Minimize distractions
- Use time-blocking for focused work
- Set daily goals
- Track accomplishments

### Wellbeing
- Take breaks to move and stretch
- Maintain social connections
- Set up ergonomic workspace
- Communicate stress or challenges to manager
