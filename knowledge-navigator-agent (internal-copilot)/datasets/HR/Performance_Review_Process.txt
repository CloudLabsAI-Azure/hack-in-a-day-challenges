# Performance Review Process

**Annual Performance Management Guide**

## Overview
Our performance review process is designed to foster continuous growth, recognize achievements, and align individual contributions with organizational goals. This guide outlines the annual review cycle, expectations, and best practices.

## Performance Review Cycle

### Timeline
- **January:** Goal setting and annual planning
- **March:** Q1 check-in and progress review
- **June:** Mid-year review and goal adjustment
- **September:** Q3 check-in and development planning
- **December:** Annual performance review and ratings

### Review Components
1. **Self-Assessment:** Employee reflection on achievements and growth areas
2. **Manager Evaluation:** Assessment of performance against goals and competencies
3. **Peer Feedback:** Input from 3-5 colleagues (optional for mid-year, required for annual)
4. **Performance Rating:** Overall rating on 5-point scale
5. **Development Plan:** Goals and growth opportunities for next period

## Performance Rating Scale

**5 - Exceptional (Top 5% of workforce)**
- Consistently exceeds all performance expectations
- Demonstrates exceptional leadership and innovation
- Significantly impacts organizational success
- Role model for company values and culture

**4 - Exceeds Expectations (20% of workforce)**
- Regularly surpasses performance goals
- Takes initiative beyond core responsibilities
- Contributes to team success and improvement
- Strong demonstration of company values

**3 - Meets Expectations (65% of workforce)**
- Consistently achieves performance goals
- Demonstrates competence in role responsibilities
- Positive contributor to team objectives
- Aligns behavior with company values

**2 - Needs Improvement (8% of workforce)**
- Inconsistently meets performance expectations
- Requires additional support and guidance
- Development plan required with specific targets
- 90-day improvement period initiated

**1 - Unsatisfactory (2% of workforce)**
- Fails to meet minimum performance standards
- Immediate performance improvement plan required
- May result in role change or separation if no improvement
- Weekly manager check-ins mandatory

## Goal Setting Framework

### SMART Goals
All performance goals should be:
- **Specific:** Clear and unambiguous objectives
- **Measurable:** Quantifiable success criteria
- **Achievable:** Realistic given resources and constraints
- **Relevant:** Aligned with team and company objectives
- **Time-bound:** Clear deadline for completion

### Goal Categories
1. **Business Results (40%):** Metrics and outcomes that drive business success
2. **Core Competencies (30%):** Skills and behaviors critical to role success
3. **Development Goals (20%):** Professional growth and skill building
4. **Values Alignment (10%):** Demonstration of company values

### Example Goals

**Business Results:**
- Increase quarterly sales revenue by 15% year-over-year
- Reduce customer support response time from 4 hours to 2 hours
- Launch new product feature by Q2 with 90% user adoption

**Core Competencies:**
- Improve presentation skills through Toastmasters participation
- Demonstrate strategic thinking by leading quarterly planning process
- Enhance technical expertise through AWS certification

**Development Goals:**
- Complete leadership development program
- Shadow senior leader for cross-functional learning
- Mentor junior team member for 6 months

## Performance Review Process Steps

### Step 1: Self-Assessment (Week 1)
**Employee Actions:**
- Review goals set at beginning of period
- Document achievements and accomplishments
- Identify challenges and areas for improvement
- Rate self on performance scale with examples
- Complete self-assessment form in performance portal

**Timeline:** Submit within 1 week of cycle start

### Step 2: Manager Evaluation (Week 2)
**Manager Actions:**
- Review employee self-assessment
- Evaluate performance against stated goals
- Gather input from stakeholders and peers
- Document specific examples of performance
- Assign preliminary performance rating
- Prepare feedback for review conversation

**Timeline:** Complete within 1 week of receiving self-assessment

### Step 3: Peer Feedback Collection (Week 2-3)
**Process:**
- Employee nominates 3-5 peers for feedback
- Manager approves peer selection
- Peers complete brief feedback survey (15 minutes)
- Feedback includes strengths and development areas
- Responses kept confidential to encourage candor

**Questions Asked:**
1. What are this person's greatest strengths?
2. What is one area for development or improvement?
3. How effectively do they collaborate with others?
4. How well do they demonstrate company values?

### Step 4: Calibration Sessions (Week 3)
**Leadership Team Actions:**
- Department leaders meet to review all ratings
- Ensure consistency and fairness across teams
- Discuss borderline ratings and challenging situations
- Validate distribution aligns with performance standards
- Confirm ratings before employee discussions

**Purpose:** Eliminate bias and ensure equitable evaluation

### Step 5: Performance Discussion (Week 4)
**Meeting Structure (60-90 minutes):**

1. **Opening (5 min):** Set positive tone, outline agenda
2. **Employee Reflection (15 min):** Employee shares self-assessment highlights
3. **Manager Feedback (20 min):** Manager shares evaluation and specific examples
4. **Discussion (20 min):** Two-way conversation about performance
5. **Rating Reveal (5 min):** Share final performance rating
6. **Future Planning (20 min):** Discuss goals and development for next period
7. **Closing (5 min):** Action items and next steps

**Best Practices:**
- Focus on behaviors and outcomes, not personality
- Provide specific examples with context
- Balance positive feedback with constructive criticism
- Listen actively and encourage dialogue
- End on forward-looking, positive note

### Step 6: Goal Setting for Next Period (Week 4-5)
**Actions:**
- Collaboratively set 4-6 goals for upcoming period
- Align individual goals with team and company objectives
- Identify development opportunities and training needs
- Document goals in performance management system
- Schedule regular check-ins (monthly or quarterly)

## Continuous Performance Management

### Regular Check-Ins
- **Weekly 1-on-1s:** Progress updates, roadblock removal, quick feedback
- **Monthly Reviews:** Goal progress assessment, priority adjustment
- **Quarterly Reviews:** Formal check-point on annual goals and development

### Real-Time Feedback
Managers should provide feedback:
- Immediately after significant achievements or concerns
- Within 24 hours of notable incidents (positive or negative)
- During weekly 1-on-1 meetings for ongoing coaching
- Via recognition platforms for peer-to-peer appreciation

### Documentation Best Practices
**What to Document:**
- Significant achievements and contributions
- Examples of values demonstration
- Customer or stakeholder feedback
- Areas needing improvement with action plans
- Training completed and skills developed

**When to Document:**
- Within 48 hours of notable events
- After major project completions
- Following customer feedback or complaints
- During monthly check-ins
- Anytime performance concerns arise

## Development Planning

### Individual Development Plan (IDP)
Every employee should have an IDP including:
- Career aspirations and goals (1-3 year horizon)
- Skills to develop for current role excellence
- Competencies needed for future career moves
- Learning resources and timelines
- Mentor or coach assignments

### Development Resources
- **Formal Training:** Internal courses, external workshops, certifications
- **Stretch Assignments:** Projects outside comfort zone
- **Job Shadowing:** Learning from others in different roles
- **Mentoring:** Pairing with experienced colleagues
- **Conferences:** Industry events for learning and networking
- **Books/Courses:** Tuition reimbursement and LinkedIn Learning

### Career Conversations
Managers should discuss:
- Career interests and aspirations
- Skills needed for advancement
- Potential growth opportunities
- Timeline for career moves
- Support needed from manager and organization

## Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

### When PIPs are Required
- Performance rating of 2 (Needs Improvement) or 1 (Unsatisfactory)
- Consistent failure to meet performance standards
- Serious performance concerns requiring immediate action
- Following informal coaching that didn't yield improvement

### PIP Components
1. **Specific Performance Issues:** Clear description of deficiencies
2. **Expected Standards:** Detailed expectations and success criteria
3. **Action Plan:** Steps employee must take to improve
4. **Support Provided:** Resources, training, coaching available
5. **Timeline:** Usually 30-90 days depending on severity
6. **Consequences:** Outcome if improvement not achieved

### PIP Process
- **Week 1:** PIP meeting, document review and sign-off
- **Weekly:** Progress check-ins with manager
- **Mid-Point:** Formal progress review and feedback
- **Final Week:** Comprehensive evaluation of improvement
- **Outcome:** Successful completion, extension, or separation

## Compensation & Rewards

### Merit Increases
- Based on annual performance rating
- Effective April 1st following December reviews
- Typical increases by rating:
  - Rating 5: 8-10% increase
  - Rating 4: 5-7% increase
  - Rating 3: 2-4% increase
  - Rating 2: 0-2% increase (discretionary)
  - Rating 1: No increase

### Bonuses & Incentives
- Annual bonus eligibility based on company and individual performance
- Target bonus percentages vary by level and role
- Paid in March following performance year
- Discretionary spot bonuses for exceptional contributions

### Promotions
- Performance rating of 3 or higher required
- Sustained high performance over 12+ months
- Demonstration of next-level competencies
- Business need and budget availability
- Competitive selection process for openings

## Manager Responsibilities

### Throughout the Year
- Provide regular feedback and coaching
- Document performance (achievements and concerns)
- Address performance issues promptly
- Support employee development goals
- Recognize and celebrate successes
- Maintain open communication

### During Review Cycle
- Complete evaluations thoroughly and on time
- Provide specific examples and evidence
- Prepare for constructive discussions
- Follow up on commitments made during review
- Update goals in performance system

### Best Practices
- Be objective and fact-based
- Avoid recency bias (review full year performance)
- Eliminate personal bias and favoritism
- Provide actionable, specific feedback
- Focus on growth and development
- Maintain confidentiality

## Employee Responsibilities

### Throughout the Year
- Actively work toward goals
- Seek feedback proactively
- Document achievements
- Communicate challenges early
- Take ownership of development
- Engage in continuous learning

### During Review Cycle
- Complete self-assessment honestly and thoroughly
- Prepare for review discussion with examples
- Engage actively in conversation
- Ask clarifying questions
- Accept feedback with openness
- Commit to development actions

### FAQs

**Q: What if I disagree with my performance rating?**
A: Discuss concerns with your manager first. If unresolved, contact HR for guidance on the appeals process.

**Q: How soon after starting am I reviewed?**
A: New hires receive 90-day review, then enter regular cycle at next annual review period.

**Q: Can my rating change after the performance discussion?**
A: Ratings are typically final after calibration, but exceptional circumstances may warrant HR review.

**Q: What if my manager leaves during the review cycle?**
A: New manager will review documentation and consult with previous manager to complete your review.

**Q: Are performance reviews tied to compensation?**
A: Yes, performance ratings directly influence merit increases and bonus eligibility.
