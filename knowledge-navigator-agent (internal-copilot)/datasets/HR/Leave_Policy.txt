# Employee Leave Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Version:** 3.2  
**Department:** Human Resources

## 1. Purpose
This policy outlines the types of leave available to employees and the procedures for requesting and approving leave.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees of the organization.

## 3. Types of Leave

### 3.1 Annual Leave
- Full-time employees accrue 15 days of annual leave per year
- Leave accrues monthly at a rate of 1.25 days per month
- Maximum carryover: 5 days to the following year
- Unused leave beyond 5 days expires on December 31st

### 3.2 Sick Leave
- Employees receive 12 days of sick leave per calendar year
- Sick leave can be used for personal illness or medical appointments
- Medical certificate required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days
- Unused sick leave does not carry over to the next year

### 3.3 Parental Leave
- Primary caregiver: 16 weeks paid leave
- Secondary caregiver: 4 weeks paid leave
- Must be taken within 12 months of birth or adoption
- Additional unpaid leave available up to 52 weeks total

### 3.4 Emergency Leave
- Up to 3 days per year for immediate family emergencies
- Requires notification to manager within 24 hours
- Documentation may be required

### 3.5 Public Holidays
- Organization observes 10 public holidays per year
- Holiday schedule published annually by HR

## 4. Leave Request Process

### 4.1 Advance Notice
- Annual leave: Minimum 2 weeks notice for requests over 5 days
- Short leave (1-2 days): Minimum 3 days notice
- Emergency situations: Notify manager as soon as possible

### 4.2 Approval Process
1. Submit leave request through employee portal
2. Manager reviews and approves/denies within 3 business days
3. Employee receives confirmation email
4. Add approved leave to team calendar

### 4.3 Peak Period Restrictions
- Leave may be restricted during peak business periods (March 1-31, September 1-30)
- Manager discretion applies based on business needs
- Early planning encouraged for peak periods

## 5. Leave Balance Management
- Check current balance through employee self-service portal
- Monthly balance statements sent via email
- HR available for balance inquiries: hr@company.com

## 6. Return to Work
- Employee must report to work on scheduled return date
- If unable to return, notify manager before 9:00 AM on return date
- Extended leave requires medical clearance for return

## 7. Leave Without Pay
- Available after all paid leave exhausted
- Requires manager and HR approval
- Maximum 30 days per calendar year
- No benefits accrual during unpaid leave

## 8. Policy Violations
- Unauthorized absence may result in disciplinary action
- Falsifying leave reasons is grounds for termination
- Repeated violations addressed through performance management
