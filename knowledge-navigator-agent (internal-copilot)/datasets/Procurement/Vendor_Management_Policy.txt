# Vendor Management & Selection Policy

**Procurement Department | Version 2.8**

## Purpose
Establish standardized procedures for selecting, onboarding, evaluating, and managing vendor relationships to ensure quality, compliance, and value.

## Vendor Selection Process

### When to Select New Vendor
- Current vendor performance inadequate
- Better pricing or terms available
- New category of goods/services needed
- Geographic expansion requiring local vendors
- Risk mitigation (diversify supplier base)
- Current vendor unable to scale with growth

### Selection Criteria

**Must-Have Requirements:**
- Financial stability (Dun & Bradstreet report)
- Adequate insurance coverage
- References from similar customers
- Compliance with industry regulations
- Quality certifications (ISO, etc.)
- Acceptable payment terms
- Reasonable lead times

**Evaluation Factors:**
- **Price (30%):** Total cost of ownership, not just unit price
- **Quality (25%):** Product/service quality, defect rates
- **Delivery (20%):** On-time performance, flexibility
- **Service (15%):** Responsiveness, support, communication
- **Innovation (10%):** Continuous improvement, technology

### Competitive Bidding

**Required For:**
- New vendor relationships >$50,000 annually
- Contract renewals with price increase >10%
- Capital expenditures >$25,000
- Professional services >$100,000

**Process:**
1. Define requirements (RFP/RFQ)
2. Identify qualified vendors (minimum 3)
3. Issue request for proposal
4. Vendor questions period (1 week)
5. Proposal submission deadline (2 weeks)
6. Evaluation by cross-functional team
7. Finalist presentations (if needed)
8. Negotiation with top choice
9. Final selection and award

**Evaluation Team:**
- Procurement lead
- Department requestor
- Finance representative
- Legal (for contracts >$100K)
- Technical expert (as needed)

### Sole Source Justification

**When Allowed:**
- Proprietary product/service (only one source)
- Compatibility required with existing systems
- Emergency situation (document and follow up)
- Regulatory or contractual requirement
- Total cost <$10,000

**Required Documentation:**
- Detailed justification
- Market research demonstrating sole source
- VP approval
- Annual review for renewals

## Vendor Onboarding

### New Vendor Setup Requirements

**Documentation:**
- Completed Vendor Application Form
- W-9 (US) or W-8BEN (International)
- Certificate of Insurance (COI)
  - General Liability: $1M per occurrence
  - Professional Liability: $1M (for services)
  - Cyber Liability: $2M (for IT/data vendors)
- Banking information for ACH payments
- Vendor terms and conditions
- Business license or registration
- References (3 minimum)

**Due Diligence:**
- D&B credit report review
- Reference checks
- Financial stability assessment
- Compliance verification
- Background check (for >$250K annually)
- Site visit (for critical vendors)

**Approval Levels:**
- <$25K annually: Procurement manager
- $25K-$100K: Director of procurement
- $100K-$500K: VP + CFO
- >$500K: Executive committee

**Timeline:** 2-3 weeks from application to approval

### Vendor Agreements

**Contract Types:**
- **Master Services Agreement (MSA):** Framework for ongoing relationship
- **Purchase Agreement:** Specific terms for goods
- **Statement of Work (SOW):** Defined project deliverables
- **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA):** Confidentiality protection

**Standard Contract Terms:**
- Scope of goods/services
- Pricing and payment terms
- Delivery schedules and lead times
- Quality standards and specifications
- Warranty and returns policy
- Insurance requirements
- Termination clauses
- Dispute resolution process
- Indemnification
- Limitation of liability

**Legal Review Required:**
- All contracts >$100,000
- Multi-year commitments
- International vendors
- High-risk categories (IT, data processing)
- Non-standard terms proposed by vendor

## Vendor Categories

### Strategic Vendors
- **Criteria:** >$500K annually, critical to operations
- **Management:** Quarterly business reviews, dedicated account team
- **Benefits:** Preferred pricing, priority service, innovation partnerships
- **Examples:** Major software vendors, logistics providers, key suppliers

### Preferred Vendors
- **Criteria:** $100K-$500K annually, good performance
- **Management:** Annual reviews, standard account support
- **Benefits:** Negotiated pricing, favorable terms
- **Examples:** Office supplies, marketing agencies, professional services

### Approved Vendors
- **Criteria:** <$100K annually, meets minimum standards
- **Management:** Performance monitoring, spot checks
- **Benefits:** Authorized for use, streamlined purchasing
- **Examples:** Specialized suppliers, occasional services

### Restricted Vendors
- **Criteria:** Performance issues, compliance concerns
- **Status:** Use discouraged or prohibited
- **Review:** Quarterly assessment for reinstatement
- **Examples:** Vendors on probation, competitors, sanctioned entities

## Vendor Performance Management

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

**Quality Metrics:**
- Defect rate (<2% target)
- Returns/rejections
- Specification compliance
- Customer satisfaction scores

**Delivery Metrics:**
- On-time delivery (>95% target)
- Complete order fulfillment
- Lead time consistency
- Damage during shipping

**Service Metrics:**
- Response time to inquiries (<24 hours)
- Issue resolution time
- Account management quality
- Communication effectiveness

**Financial Metrics:**
- Invoice accuracy (>98% target)
- Pricing compliance
- Payment disputes
- Cost savings delivered

### Performance Reviews

**Monthly Monitoring:**
- Automated scorecard generation
- Exception reports for issues
- Trend analysis
- Alert procurement of problems

**Quarterly Business Reviews (Strategic Vendors):**
- Performance scorecard review
- Discussion of issues and improvements
- Upcoming needs and forecasts
- Innovation opportunities
- Relationship strengthening

**Annual Evaluations (All Vendors):**
- Comprehensive performance assessment
- Contract renewal recommendations
- Pricing negotiations
- Risk assessment
- Continued authorization decision

### Performance Rating Scale

**5 - Exceptional (Top 10%):**
- Exceeds all KPIs
- Proactive problem-solving
- Continuous innovation
- Strategic partnership value

**4 - Exceeds Expectations (20%):**
- Consistently meets/exceeds KPIs
- Responsive and reliable
- Good value delivery

**3 - Meets Expectations (60%):**
- Generally meets KPIs
- Acceptable performance
- Some improvement areas

**2 - Needs Improvement (8%):**
- Frequently misses KPIs
- Performance plan required
- 90-day probation period

**1 - Unacceptable (2%):**
- Consistently fails KPIs
- Immediate termination recommended
- Find alternative vendor

## Vendor Relationship Management

### Communication Protocols

**Regular Touchpoints:**
- Strategic vendors: Monthly calls
- Preferred vendors: Quarterly check-ins
- Approved vendors: As-needed basis
- All vendors: Annual survey

**Escalation Path:**
1. Day-to-day: Vendor account manager
2. Service issues: Procurement specialist
3. Contract disputes: Procurement manager
4. Unresolved: Director of procurement
5. Critical: VP + legal

**Vendor Portal:**
- Access: vendors.company.com
- Features: Order status, invoicing, performance metrics, document repository
- Training provided during onboarding

### Issue Management

**Vendor Performance Issues:**
1. Document specific problems
2. Communicate concerns to vendor
3. Set expectations for improvement
4. Implement corrective action plan
5. Follow up and monitor progress
6. Escalate if no improvement

**Service Recovery:**
- Root cause analysis
- Corrective actions defined
- Timeline for resolution
- Compensation if appropriate (credits, discounts)
- Prevention measures
- Relationship repair

**Termination Process:**
1. Review contract termination clauses
2. Document performance failures
3. Provide written notice (typically 30-90 days)
4. Transition to alternative vendor
5. Final invoice reconciliation
6. Lessons learned documentation

## Risk Management

### Vendor Risk Assessment

**Risk Categories:**
- **Financial:** Credit risk, bankruptcy potential
- **Operational:** Single source, capacity constraints
- **Compliance:** Regulatory violations, ethical issues
- **Cybersecurity:** Data breaches, IT vulnerabilities
- **Geopolitical:** International instability, sanctions
- **Reputational:** Bad publicity, lawsuits

**Risk Levels:**
- **High:** Critical vendor, >$1M annually, sole source
- **Medium:** Important vendor, $250K-$1M, limited alternatives
- **Low:** Non-critical, <$250K, many alternatives

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Diversify supplier base (multiple vendors per category)
- Maintain safety stock for critical items
- Contractual protections (SLAs, penalties)
- Business continuity plans
- Cybersecurity requirements
- Insurance requirements

### Vendor Audits

**When Required:**
- Strategic vendors (annual)
- High-risk vendors (as needed)
- Quality issues identified
- Compliance verification
- Contract renewal

**Audit Types:**
- Financial audit (stability verification)
- Quality audit (process and controls)
- Compliance audit (regulatory adherence)
- Security audit (IT and data protection)
- Facility inspection (for manufacturing)

**Conducted By:**
- Internal audit team
- Third-party auditors
- Procurement specialists
- Quality assurance team

## Compliance & Ethics

### Code of Conduct for Vendors

**Expected Standards:**
- Ethical business practices
- Legal and regulatory compliance
- Fair labor practices
- Environmental responsibility
- No bribery or corruption
- Respect for intellectual property
- Confidentiality and data protection

**Prohibited Practices:**
- Child labor or forced labor
- Discrimination or harassment
- Unsafe working conditions
- Environmental violations
- Bribery or kickbacks
- Conflicts of interest

### Conflict of Interest

**Disclosure Required:**
- Employee relationship with vendor
- Financial interest in vendor
- Family member employed by vendor
- Gifts >$50 received from vendor
- Entertainment >$100 from vendor

**Report To:** procurement-ethics@company.com

### Gifts & Entertainment

**Acceptable:**
- Promotional items <$25 (pens, notepads)
- Occasional business meals <$100
- Industry conference attendance

**Prohibited:**
- Cash or gift cards
- Personal gifts >$50
- Lavish entertainment
- Vacation or travel
- Gifts during contract negotiations

## Vendor Diversity Program

### Goals
- 15% spend with minority-owned businesses
- 10% spend with women-owned businesses
- 5% spend with veteran-owned businesses
- 5% spend with local/small businesses

### Diverse Vendor Certification
- Verify certifications (MBE, WBE, VBE, SBE)
- Include in RFP distributions
- Track spend by category
- Report quarterly to executive team

### Benefits
- Strengthen local communities
- Support economic development
- Innovation from diverse perspectives
- Positive brand reputation
- Potential tax benefits
