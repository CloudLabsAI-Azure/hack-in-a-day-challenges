# Purchase Order Request Process

**Procurement Department Guide | Version 3.5**

## What is a Purchase Order (PO)?

A Purchase Order is a formal document issued to vendors authorizing a purchase transaction. It includes item details, quantities, prices, payment terms, and delivery requirements. POs create legally binding contracts when accepted by vendors.

## When PO is Required

### Always Require PO:
- Purchases over $1,000
- Recurring vendor relationships
- Multi-item orders
- Custom manufacturing or services
- Software licenses or subscriptions
- Professional services contracts
- Equipment and hardware
- Marketing and promotional materials
- Office furniture and supplies (bulk)

### PO Not Required:
- Purchases under $1,000 (use corporate card)
- Utilities and facility costs (auto-paid)
- Employee expenses (use expense reports)
- Emergency purchases (followed by PO within 24 hours)
- Pre-approved contracts with blanket POs

## Purchase Order Types

### Standard PO
- **Use For:** One-time purchases with specific quantities and delivery dates
- **Details:** Itemized list, unit prices, total amount, delivery date
- **Example:** Office furniture order, computer equipment, marketing materials

### Blanket PO
- **Use For:** Recurring purchases from same vendor over period (usually 1 year)
- **Details:** Maximum spend amount, validity period, approved items/services
- **Example:** Office supplies, IT support services, cleaning services
- **Benefits:** Reduces paperwork for frequent purchases

### Contract PO
- **Use For:** Long-term agreements with negotiated terms and pricing
- **Details:** Multi-year commitments, volume discounts, service level agreements
- **Example:** Software licenses, managed services, equipment leasing

### Planned PO
- **Use For:** Known future need with flexible delivery schedule
- **Details:** Quantities estimated, delivery dates TBD, pricing locked
- **Example:** Quarterly marketing campaigns, seasonal inventory

## PO Request Process

### Step 1: Prepare Your Request

**Gather Information:**
- Detailed description of goods/services needed
- Vendor information (name, contact, address)
- Itemized list with part numbers/SKUs
- Quantity needed
- Unit price and total cost
- Requested delivery date
- Business justification
- Budget code/cost center
- Manager pre-approval (if required)

**Vendor Selection:**
- Get quotes from 3 vendors for purchases >$10,000
- Use preferred vendor list when possible
- New vendors require procurement approval
- Include vendor W-9 form for first-time vendors

### Step 2: Check Budget Availability

1. Log in to financial system: finance.company.com
2. Review budget vs. actuals for your cost center
3. Confirm sufficient budget for purchase
4. If over budget, request budget amendment first
5. Note budget code for PO request

### Step 3: Submit PO Request

**Via Procurement Portal (Preferred):**
1. Go to procurement.company.com
2. Click "Create Purchase Request"
3. Select PO type (Standard, Blanket, Contract)
4. Fill in all required fields:
   - Vendor details
   - Item descriptions
   - Quantities and unit prices
   - Delivery date and location
   - Budget code
   - Business justification
5. Attach supporting documents:
   - Vendor quotes (required for >$10,000)
   - Technical specifications
   - Statement of Work (for services)
   - Comparisons (if multiple vendors)
6. Submit request

**Via Email (If Portal Unavailable):**
- Email: procurement@company.com
- Subject: "PO Request - [Your Name] - [Brief Description]"
- Include all details listed above
- Attach vendor quotes and specifications

### Step 4: Approval Workflow

**Automatic Routing Based on Amount:**

**< $5,000:**
1. Manager approval (1 day)
2. Procurement review (1 day)
3. PO issued (1 day)
**Total: 2-3 days**

**$5,000 - $25,000:**
1. Manager approval
2. Director approval
3. Procurement review
4. Finance approval (budget confirmation)
5. PO issued
**Total: 3-5 days**

**$25,000 - $100,000:**
1. Manager approval
2. Director approval
3. VP approval
4. Procurement competitive bid review
5. Legal review (if new vendor or contract)
6. CFO approval
7. PO issued
**Total: 1-2 weeks**

**> $100,000:**
1. Full approval chain above
2. Executive committee review
3. Board notification (if >$500K)
4. Formal vendor selection process
5. Contract negotiation
6. PO issued
**Total: 2-4 weeks**

### Step 5: PO Issuance

**Procurement Actions:**
- Verify all approvals received
- Validate vendor information
- Check for duplicate orders
- Assign PO number (format: PO-2024-XXXXX)
- Generate PO document
- Send PO to vendor via email
- Copy requestor and approvers
- Update procurement system

**PO Document Includes:**
- Unique PO number
- Issue date
- Vendor name and address
- Ship-to address
- Billing address
- Item descriptions and quantities
- Unit prices and total amount
- Payment terms
- Delivery date
- Special instructions
- Terms and conditions

### Step 6: Order Tracking

**Requestor Responsibilities:**
- Monitor order status in portal
- Follow up with vendor if delays
- Coordinate delivery logistics
- Notify procurement of any changes
- Receive and inspect goods
- Submit receipt confirmation

**Track Your Order:**
- Portal: procurement.company.com/track
- Email updates sent automatically
- Contact procurement with PO number

## Receiving & Three-Way Match

### Goods Receipt Process

**When Delivery Arrives:**
1. Verify PO number on packing slip
2. Count and inspect items
3. Check for damage
4. Compare to PO (quantities, items)
5. Sign delivery receipt
6. Report discrepancies immediately

**Log Receipt in System:**
1. Go to procurement.company.com
2. Find your PO
3. Click "Record Receipt"
4. Enter received quantities
5. Note any damages or shortages
6. Upload photos if damaged
7. Submit receipt confirmation

**Partial Receipts:**
- Receive what arrived
- Note items still pending
- PO stays open until complete
- Follow up with vendor on back-ordered items

### Three-Way Match

**Before Payment, Verify:**
1. **Purchase Order** - What was authorized
2. **Receipt** - What was actually received
3. **Invoice** - What vendor is billing

**All Three Must Match:**
- Item descriptions
- Quantities
- Prices
- Total amounts

**If Discrepancy:**
- Hold payment
- Contact vendor
- Resolve before paying
- Procurement assists with disputes

## PO Changes & Cancellations

### Change Orders

**Allowed Changes:**
- Quantity adjustments (within 20%)
- Delivery date changes
- Ship-to address updates
- Price corrections (with vendor agreement)

**Process:**
1. Contact procurement with PO number
2. Explain needed changes
3. Procurement coordinates with vendor
4. Revised PO issued (new revision number)
5. All parties notified

**Requires Re-Approval:**
- Total amount increase >10%
- Scope changes
- Vendor substitution
- Budget code changes

### Cancellations

**Valid Reasons:**
- No longer needed
- Vendor cannot deliver
- Found better pricing elsewhere
- Budget no longer available
- Project cancelled

**Process:**
1. Contact procurement immediately
2. Provide PO number and reason
3. Procurement contacts vendor
4. Confirm cancellation in writing
5. Check for cancellation fees
6. Close PO in system

**Cancellation Fees:**
- May apply if vendor started work
- Custom orders often non-refundable
- Review vendor terms before ordering
- Manager approval if fee >$500

## Vendor Management

### Preferred Vendor List

**Benefits of Preferred Vendors:**
- Pre-negotiated pricing
- Faster processing
- Established quality standards
- Reliable delivery
- Simplified paperwork

**Access List:**
- Portal: procurement.company.com/vendors
- Organized by category
- Contact information included
- Negotiated terms listed

### New Vendor Requests

**Required Before First PO:**
1. Complete Vendor Information Form
2. Provide W-9 (US) or W-8 (International)
3. Certificate of Insurance (if required)
4. Banking information for ACH
5. References (for vendors >$50K annually)
6. Background check (for high-value vendors)

**Procurement Reviews:**
- Financial stability
- Reputation and references
- Insurance coverage
- Terms and conditions
- Pricing competitiveness

**Timeline:** 7-10 business days for approval

### Vendor Performance

**Tracked Metrics:**
- On-time delivery rate
- Quality of goods/services
- Responsiveness
- Invoice accuracy
- Issue resolution

**Quarterly Reviews:**
- Poor performers flagged
- Alternative vendors identified
- Continuous improvement discussions
- Contracts renegotiated or terminated

## Purchase Categories

### Office Supplies & Materials
- **Preferred Vendor:** Office Depot (blanket PO)
- **Process:** Order online, auto-approved up to $500/order
- **Delivery:** Next business day
- **No PO Required:** Individual items <$100

### Technology & Software
- **Requires:** IT approval before procurement
- **Process:** Submit request to IT first
- **Security Review:** For new software vendors
- **Licensing:** Track user counts and renewals

### Professional Services
- **Requires:** Statement of Work (SOW)
- **Process:** Define deliverables and timeline
- **Payment:** Often milestone-based
- **Examples:** Consultants, contractors, agencies

### Marketing & Advertising
- **Requires:** Marketing manager approval
- **Process:** Campaign details and ROI projections
- **Vendors:** Agencies, printers, media buyers
- **Budget:** Separate marketing budget codes

### Facilities & Maintenance
- **Vendors:** Pre-approved service providers
- **Process:** Facilities manager coordinates
- **Emergency:** 24-hour response available
- **Blanket POs:** For routine maintenance

## Compliance & Controls

### Segregation of Duties
- **Requestor:** Cannot approve own PO
- **Approver:** Cannot be requestor
- **Receiver:** Should not be requestor (when possible)
- **Payment:** Processed by Finance, not requestor

### Audit Trail
**All Actions Logged:**
- Who requested PO
- Who approved at each level
- When PO was issued
- Receipt confirmation
- Invoice matched
- Payment made

### Conflict of Interest
**Disclosure Required:**
- Personal relationship with vendor
- Family member owns vendor
- Financial interest in vendor company
- Gifts or incentives from vendor

**Report To:** procurement-ethics@company.com

### Fraud Prevention
**Red Flags:**
- Rush orders without justification
- Splitting purchases to avoid approval limits
- Unusual vendor requests
- Vendor contact changes
- Duplicate invoices

**Report Suspicious Activity:**
- Email: procurement-fraud@company.com
- Phone: (555) 123-4900
- Confidential reporting available

## Procurement Best Practices

### Plan Ahead
- Submit requests 2-4 weeks before needed
- Account for approval time and delivery
- Don't wait until last minute
- Communicate urgent needs early

### Be Specific
- Detailed item descriptions
- Part numbers and specifications
- Exact quantities needed
- Preferred delivery date

### Get Competitive Quotes
- 3 quotes for purchases >$10,000
- Document why preferred vendor chosen
- Consider total cost (shipping, support, warranty)

### Leverage Volume
- Combine orders when possible
- Coordinate with other departments
- Negotiate better pricing for bulk
- Use blanket POs for recurring needs

### Build Relationships
- Communicate clearly with vendors
- Pay invoices promptly
- Provide feedback (positive and negative)
- Partner for continuous improvement
